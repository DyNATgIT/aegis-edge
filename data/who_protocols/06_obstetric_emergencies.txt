SOURCE: WHO Obstetric Emergency Management
CATEGORY: Obstetric and Maternal Emergencies
AUTHORITY: World Health Organization Managing Complications in Pregnancy and Childbirth

============================================================
OBSTETRIC TRIAGE PRINCIPLES
============================================================

ANY PREGNANT WOMAN WITH THE FOLLOWING = IMMEDIATE:
  Uncontrolled hemorrhage (antepartum or postpartum)
  Seizures (eclampsia)
  Unconsciousness or severely altered mental status
  Severe respiratory distress
  Shock (any cause)
  Signs of placental abruption with fetal distress
  Cord prolapse
  Shoulder dystocia
  Uterine rupture

GESTATIONAL AGE ESTIMATION (if unknown):
  Fundal height in cm ≈ gestational age in weeks (after 20 weeks)
  Fundal height at umbilicus ≈ 20 weeks
  Fundal height at xiphisternum ≈ 36 weeks
  If > 20 weeks gestation: consider both maternal AND fetal status

============================================================
POSTPARTUM HEMORRHAGE — MOST COMMON OBSTETRIC EMERGENCY
============================================================

DEFINITION: Blood loss > 500 mL vaginal delivery, > 1000 mL caesarean

RECOGNITION: Uterus boggy/soft (not contracted), heavy vaginal bleeding
TRIAGE: IMMEDIATE

CAUSES (4 T's):
  TONE (uterine atony) — 80% of cases: Uterus fails to contract after delivery
  TRAUMA: Lacerations, hematoma
  TISSUE: Retained placenta or clots
  THROMBIN: Coagulation disorder

IMMEDIATE MANAGEMENT:
  1. Bimanual uterine massage — compress uterus between hands
  2. Oxytocin 10 IU IM immediately after delivery (prophylaxis)
     Or 20-40 IU in 1L saline IV over 4 hours (treatment)
  3. Large bore IV access × 2, urgent fluid resuscitation
  4. Tranexamic acid 1g IV within 3 hours if hemorrhage > 500 mL
  5. Urgent manual removal of retained placenta if suspected
  6. If bleeding persists: uterine compression sutures, surgical intervention

============================================================
ECLAMPSIA — HYPERTENSIVE CRISIS IN PREGNANCY
============================================================

PREECLAMPSIA: BP > 140/90 + proteinuria after 20 weeks
SEVERE PREECLAMPSIA: BP > 160/110 or end-organ damage
ECLAMPSIA: Seizures in preeclamptic patient

TRIAGE: ALL eclampsia = IMMEDIATE
         Severe preeclampsia = IMMEDIATE

ECLAMPSIA MANAGEMENT:
  1. Seizure: Position left lateral, protect airway, O2
  2. Magnesium sulfate: 4-6 g IV over 15-20 minutes (loading dose)
     Then: 1-2 g/hour IV maintenance
     CRITICAL: Monitor for toxicity — absent patellar reflexes,
               respiratory depression, urine output < 25 mL/hour
     Antidote: Calcium gluconate 1g IV over 10 minutes
  3. BP control: Hydralazine 5-10 mg IV q20min OR Labetalol 20mg IV
     Target: 140-155/90-105 (avoid over-lowering — fetal compromise)
  4. Delivery: Definitive treatment — organize urgent delivery

MAGNESIUM TOXICITY SIGNS:
  Loss of patellar reflex (earliest sign)
  Respiratory depression (serious)
  Cardiac arrest (serum Mg > 15 mEq/L)
  Treatment: Stop MgSO4, give calcium gluconate 1g IV
